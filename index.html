LTX-2

The first complete open-source audio-video foundation model

LTX-2 marks a category-defining milestone: the first production-ready model combining native audio and video generation with fully open weights, training code, and inference code. Its 19B-parameter Asymmetric Dual-Stream Diffusion Transformer uses 14B for the video stream and 5B for audio, with bidirectional cross-attention layers synchronizing both modalities in a single unified pass. Generates up to 20 seconds of synchronized audio-video at native 4K and 50 fps on consumer hardware. In human preference studies it performs comparably to Sora 2 and Veo 3, while significantly outperforming all open-source alternatives. Announced with NVIDIA at CES 2026 â€” Lightricks' "DeepSeek moment."

LTX-Video

The world's first real-time open-source video generation model

LTXV launched as the first open-source video diffusion model capable of generating video faster than real-time. At 2B parameters it ran on consumer GPUs, introducing motion consistency techniques that reduced visual artifacts common in video diffusion. Its tight integration between Video-VAE and denoising transformer â€” with a 1:192 spatiotemporal compression ratio â€” enabled full spatiotemporal self-attention across all frames simultaneously. Available on HuggingFace and GitHub, it reached 9,300+ stars and seeded a global developer community. Trained on Google Cloud TPUs, cutting a six-month timeline to four.
